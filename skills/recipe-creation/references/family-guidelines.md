# Family Dietary Guidelines and Preferences

Reference for the Johnson family's dietary requirements, preferences, and cooking defaults.

---

## Household

- **Size**: 6 people
- **Children**: Yes — multiple younger children
- **Lifestyle**: Homesteading family — scratch cooking, whole foods, seasonal eating
- **Priority**: Nourishing, real food over convenience; quality fats and proteins

---

## Hard Rules (Non-Negotiable)

### NO PORK

Absolutely no pork or pork-derived products in any recipe.

**Forbidden:**
- Pork (all cuts: chops, loin, tenderloin, shoulder, belly)
- Bacon
- Ham
- Lard (pork-rendered fat — beef tallow is fine)
- Prosciutto, pancetta, guanciale
- Salami, pepperoni (unless explicitly beef/turkey-based)
- Pork sausage (Italian, breakfast, bratwurst — unless beef/turkey-based)
- Chorizo (unless explicitly beef or chicken-based)
- Carnitas
- Pork ribs
- Pork rinds / chicharrones
- Fatback
- Pulled pork

**Compliant alternatives:**
- Beef (chuck, short ribs, brisket, ground, sirloin)
- Lamb (shoulder, leg, ground, chops)
- Chicken (thighs preferred for braises; breasts for quick cooking)
- Turkey (ground turkey works well as a pork sausage substitute)
- Duck (legs and thighs are especially good for slow cooking)
- Fish and shellfish

---

### NO SEED OILS

No industrially processed seed or vegetable oils.

**Forbidden:**
- Vegetable oil (generic)
- Canola oil
- Sunflower oil
- Safflower oil
- Corn oil
- Soybean oil
- Cottonseed oil
- Grapeseed oil
- Rice bran oil
- Peanut oil (borderline — prefer to avoid)
- Shortening (if made from seed oils — most commercial shortenings are)
- Margarine (almost always seed-oil based)
- Any oil labeled "refined" or "hydrogenated" from the above sources

**Compliant fats:**
| Fat | Best Use | Notes |
|-----|----------|-------|
| Avocado oil | High-heat searing, roasting, stir-fry | Neutral flavor, high smoke point (~520°F) |
| Tallow (beef) | High-heat searing, frying, roasting | Rich flavor, excellent for beef dishes |
| Ghee | Sautéing, finishing, South Asian dishes | Clarified butter — no milk solids |
| Butter | Sautéing, finishing, baking | Unsalted preferred for cooking |
| Olive oil | Dressings, low-heat sautéing, finishing | Extra virgin for flavor; regular for cooking |
| Coconut oil | Baking, certain Asian dishes | Adds distinct flavor — use intentionally |
| Duck fat | Roasting vegetables, sautéing | Exceptional flavor for potatoes and root vegetables |

## Soft Rules (Default Preferences)

### Scale for 6

Default all recipes to serve **6 people**. Set `servings: 6` unless the recipe is clearly for a different count (e.g., a 1-loaf bread recipe).

When scaling existing recipes:
- Scale ingredient quantities proportionally
- Note that braising and baking times do not scale with quantity
- For baked goods: scale within a single batch; multiple batches are better than one oversized batch

### Mild to Moderate Heat

The family includes younger children. Default to mild-to-moderate heat levels.

**Guidelines:**
- **No habanero, scotch bonnet, or bird's eye chili** — too hot for kids
- **Ghost pepper, Carolina Reaper** — absolutely not
- **Jalapeño**: fine, but **remove seeds and ribs** to reduce heat; add a note in the recipe
- **Serrano**: use sparingly, remove seeds
- **Chipotle (dried/canned)**: mild-to-medium heat, smoky — generally fine in moderate quantity
- **Ancho chili**: mild, fruity — excellent
- **Dried red pepper flakes**: fine in moderation (up to 1/2 tsp for a 6-serving dish)
- **Black pepper**: fine
- **Cayenne**: use minimally (≤1/4 tsp for a 6-serving dish)

When a user requests "spicy," clarify and target medium heat — not restaurant-level hot.

### Whole Food Preference

Prefer whole, minimally processed ingredients:
- Whole grains over refined where possible (but white flour for bread is totally fine)
- Real butter over dairy substitutes
- Whole milk over skim
- Fresh herbs where practical; dried are fine
- Bone-in cuts for braises (more collagen = more gelatin = richer sauce)
- Homemade stock preferred; store-bought fine as backup

### Seasonal Thinking

When suggesting or creating recipes, consider the season:
- **Winter**: braised meats, root vegetables, soups, stews, hearty breads
- **Summer**: grilled meats, fresh tomatoes, cucumbers, light salads, cold desserts
- **Fall**: squash, apples, root vegetables, warm spices, harvest-style dishes
- **Spring**: asparagus, greens, lighter proteins, fresh herbs

## Pantry Staples (Usually On Hand)

**Fats**: avocado oil, tallow, butter, ghee, olive oil
**Proteins**: beef (various cuts), chicken (whole birds and thighs), eggs
**Grains**: bread flour, all-purpose flour, whole wheat flour, rolled oats, rice
**Legumes**: dried beans (black, pinto, kidney), lentils
**Aromatics**: yellow onions, garlic, carrots, celery, ginger
**Spices**: kosher salt, black pepper, cumin, coriander, paprika, smoked paprika, chili powder, oregano, thyme, rosemary, bay leaves, cinnamon, turmeric
**Acids**: apple cider vinegar, red wine vinegar, lemon juice
**Stocks**: homemade beef stock and chicken stock (when available)
**Tomatoes**: canned whole tomatoes, tomato paste, canned diced tomatoes
**Dairy**: whole milk, heavy cream, sour cream, cheddar cheese, Parmesan
