# Dietary Rules — Complete Reference

Full specification of the Johnson family's dietary guidelines.

---

## Forbidden: Pork and Pork-Derived Products

No exceptions. All items in this list are forbidden regardless of preparation, form, or cuisine.

### Whole Cuts and Preparations
- Pork chops (all cuts)
- Pork loin and tenderloin
- Pork shoulder (Boston butt, picnic)
- Pork belly
- Pork ribs (baby back, spare)
- Pulled pork
- Carnitas (traditional — pork braised in lard/fat)
- Pork roast

### Cured and Processed
- Bacon (all varieties)
- Ham (cooked, cured, smoked, fresh)
- Prosciutto di Parma and similar aged hams
- Pancetta
- Guanciale
- Salami (most commercial salami is pork unless labeled otherwise)
- Pepperoni (traditional — usually pork/beef blend; beef-only versions exist)
- Chorizo (Spanish/Mexican — usually pork; beef and chicken versions exist)
- Soppressata
- Capicola / coppa
- Mortadella

### Ground and Sausage Forms
- Ground pork
- Pork sausage (Italian sausage, breakfast sausage, bratwurst, kielbasa — unless explicitly labeled beef or turkey)
- Andouille (traditional — pork; chicken andouille exists)

### Rendered and Extracted
- Lard (rendered pork fat — beef tallow is compliant)
- Fatback
- Pork rinds / chicharrones
- Pork gelatin (check ingredient labels on gelatin-based products)

### Hidden Sources
- Many commercial stocks and broths contain pork gelatin — check labels
- Some bread products use lard — check bakery items
- Refried beans (traditional) are often cooked in lard

## Forbidden: Seed and Industrial Vegetable Oils

No industrially extracted seed or grain oils.

### Clearly Forbidden Oils
- Vegetable oil (generic label)
- Canola oil (rapeseed oil)
- Sunflower oil
- Safflower oil
- Corn oil
- Soybean oil
- Cottonseed oil
- Grapeseed oil
- Rice bran oil

### Processed Forms
- Margarine (virtually all commercial margarine is seed-oil based)
- Shortening (most commercial shortenings — Crisco, etc.)
- Hydrogenated vegetable oil
- Partially hydrogenated oil (any source)

### Borderline / Context-Dependent
- **Peanut oil**: Prefer to avoid; use avocado oil or tallow instead. If a recipe specifically requires peanut oil for a dish where it is traditional (Thai, certain Chinese cooking) and there is no viable substitute, it may be used minimally — but note it.
- **Sesame oil**: Seed-derived but used in small finishing quantities in Asian cuisine. Use minimally as a flavor agent in very small amounts (1 tsp for an entire dish); do not use as a cooking oil.

### Hidden Seed Oil Sources
- Store-bought broth, stocks, and bouillon often contain soybean or canola oil — check labels
- Mayonnaise (nearly all commercial mayo is made with canola or soybean oil — make homemade with avocado oil)
- Store-bought salad dressings — almost universally seed-oil based
- Crackers and bread products — often contain "vegetable oil"
- Restaurant food — assume seed oils unless told otherwise
- Canned fish (check oil type — some use soybean oil)

## Compliant Fats — Full Reference

| Fat | Smoke Point | Best Uses | Flavor |
|-----|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Avocado oil | ~520°F | High-heat searing, roasting, stir-fry, all-purpose | Neutral to mild buttery |
| Tallow (beef) | ~400°F | High-heat searing, frying, roasting beef | Rich, beefy |
| Ghee | ~485°F | Sautéing, Indian/South Asian cuisine, finishing | Rich butter flavor, no dairy |
| Butter (unsalted) | ~300°F | Sautéing aromatics, baking, finishing, sauces | Classic dairy richness |
| Olive oil (EVOO) | ~375°F | Dressings, low-heat sautéing, finishing drizzle | Fruity, grassy, peppery |
| Olive oil (regular) | ~465°F | Medium-high sautéing, roasting | Mild olive flavor |
| Coconut oil | ~350°F | Baking, certain Asian dishes, medium-heat cooking | Distinct coconut; use intentionally |
| Duck fat | ~375°F | Roasting potatoes and root vegetables, sautéing | Exceptionally rich and savory |

## Heat Level Guidelines

### Mild (Kid-Safe)
- Black pepper: fine at any reasonable level
- Paprika (sweet or smoked): fine
- Ancho chili powder: mild, fruity — fine
- Cumin, coriander, turmeric: not hot at all
- Mild chili powder blends: fine
- Chipotle in very small amounts (1/4 tsp per 6 servings): mild smokiness

### Moderate (Acceptable with Note)
- Jalapeño: fine when **seeded and deveined**; in the recipe, note "jalapeño, seeded"
- Chipotle (1/2 tsp per 6 servings): medium smokiness and heat
- Red pepper flakes: fine up to 1/2 tsp per 6 servings
- Cayenne: fine up to 1/4 tsp per 6 servings
- Serrano: acceptable when seeded and used sparingly (half a serrano per 6 servings)

### Hot (Flag and Adjust)
- Jalapeño with seeds → flag, recommend seeding
- Cayenne > 1/2 tsp per 6 servings → flag, reduce
- Red pepper flakes > 1 tsp per 6 servings → flag, reduce
- Serrano unseeded → flag, recommend seeding or omitting

### Forbidden (Too Hot for Kids)
- Habanero
- Scotch bonnet
- Bird's eye chili (Thai chili)
- Ghost pepper
- Carolina Reaper
- Any variety rated above ~50,000 Scoville

## Scaling Guidance

Default all recipes to **6 servings**. When scaling an existing recipe:

| Original Servings | Scale Factor | Notes |
|-------------------|--------------|-------|
| 2 | 3x | Triple all quantities |
| 4 | 1.5x | Increase by half |
| 8 | 0.75x | Reduce by quarter |
| 12 | 0.5x | Halve all quantities |

**Timing does not scale with quantity:**
- Braising time remains the same regardless of how much meat is in the pot
- Baking time remains the same for the same pan size; may increase slightly for larger volumes
- Stock simmering time is the same for small or large batches

**Temperature never scales** — always cook to the same internal temperatures and visual cues.
