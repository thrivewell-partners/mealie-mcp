# Dietary Substitution Guide

Compliant replacements for forbidden ingredients.

---

## Oil Substitutions

### For Neutral High-Heat Cooking (Searing, Stir-Fry, Deep Fry)

| Forbidden | Substitute | Ratio | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|-------|
| Canola oil | Avocado oil | 1:1 | Identical behavior, neutral flavor |
| Vegetable oil | Avocado oil | 1:1 | Same neutrality, much higher smoke point |
| Sunflower oil | Avocado oil or tallow | 1:1 | Tallow adds beef richness; avocado stays neutral |
| Corn oil | Avocado oil | 1:1 | No flavor difference |
| Grapeseed oil | Avocado oil | 1:1 | Grapeseed has neutral flavor — avocado matches it |

### For Roasting and Oven Cooking

| Forbidden | Substitute | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Vegetable oil (roasting vegetables) | Avocado oil or tallow | Tallow adds depth to root vegetables |
| Canola oil (sheet pan) | Avocado oil | Direct swap |
| Shortening (frying) | Tallow | Better flavor, similar performance |

### For Baking

| Forbidden | Substitute | Ratio | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|-------|
| Vegetable oil (cakes, muffins) | Melted butter | 1:1 by volume | Adds dairy flavor; moisture slightly different |
| Canola oil (baking) | Melted coconut oil | 1:1 | Adds slight coconut flavor; use refined if unwanted |
| Shortening (biscuits, pastry) | Butter or tallow | 1:1 | Butter: more flavor; tallow: flakier pastry |
| Margarine | Butter | 1:1 | Always prefer butter |

### For Dressings and Finishing

| Forbidden | Substitute | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Vegetable oil (dressing) | Extra virgin olive oil | Better flavor |
| Canola oil (mayo base) | Avocado oil | Make mayo at home with avocado oil + egg yolk |
| Soybean oil (commercial mayo) | Homemade avocado oil mayo | Control all ingredients |

## Pork Substitutions

### Bacon

Bacon's functional roles: smokiness, rendered fat, salt, crispy texture (sometimes).

| Application | Substitute | Method |
|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Smoky depth in soups/braises | Smoked paprika (1/2 tsp per 4 strips) + tallow | Add smoked paprika when blooming spices |
| Crispy topping | Beef bacon (exists — check specialty butchers) | Cook the same way |
| Fat for cooking (rendered bacon fat) | Tallow or butter | Equal substitution for cooking fat |
| Salty pork bits in salads | Skip, or use beef bresaola finely diced | Bresaola is air-dried beef |

### Ham

| Application | Substitute | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Ham in split pea soup | Turkey thigh (cooked, diced) | Similar mildness and texture |
| Ham in bean soups | Smoked turkey leg or wing | Same smoking/flavoring role |
| Diced ham in casseroles | Diced cooked chicken thigh | Mild, tender |
| Ham hocks for smoking beans | Smoked beef knuckle or beef shank | Ask a butcher; imparts similar smokiness |

### Pork Sausage (Ground)

| Application | Substitute | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Italian sausage | Ground beef + fennel seed (1 tsp/lb) + garlic + paprika | Season to match; the spice blend is what makes it Italian |
| Breakfast sausage | Ground turkey + sage + black pepper + pinch of nutmeg | Turkey responds well to these spices |
| Chorizo | Ground beef + ancho chili powder + cumin + smoked paprika + garlic + apple cider vinegar | Or purchase beef chorizo from a butcher or specialty store |
| Bratwurst | Ground beef or turkey + white pepper + ginger + nutmeg + mustard seed | |

### Prosciutto / Pancetta / Guanciale

These are Italian cured pork products used to add fat and salt, often as a base for pasta sauces.

| Application | Substitute | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Pancetta in pasta sauce base | Skip it; use butter or olive oil + a pinch of salt | The pasta sauce does not need pork fat to be great |
| Prosciutto-wrapped items | Skip the wrap; season the item with salt instead | |
| Guanciale in carbonara | Skip pork entirely; use a good olive oil finish + extra Parmesan | Traditional carbonara requires pork; make a compliant variation |

### Lard

| Application | Substitute | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Lard in pastry/pie crust | Tallow (1:1) or butter (1:1) | Tallow makes flakier pastry; butter adds flavor |
| Lard for frying | Tallow | Better flavor, similar performance |
| Lard for tamales | Tallow (beef) | Traditional tamales use lard; tallow works well |
| Lard as cooking fat | Avocado oil, tallow, or ghee | Any compliant fat |

### Carnitas

Carnitas is slow-braised pork finished with high heat. The technique is the same with beef.

**Beef carnitas approach:**
- Use beef chuck (the most forgiving, fatty cut)
- Braise in: orange juice, lime juice, cumin, ancho chili, garlic, beef stock
- 3–4 hours at 300°F covered
- Spread on a sheet pan, broil 5–8 minutes until edges crisp

## Heat Reduction Substitutions

| Too Hot Ingredient | Compliant Version | Notes |
|--------------------|------------------|-------|
| Habanero | Ancho chili + small amount of jalapeño | Ancho adds fruity depth without heat; jalapeño adds mild heat |
| Scotch bonnet | Ancho or mulato chili | Deep flavor without extreme heat |
| Bird's eye chili | Seeded jalapeño or fresh serrano (half) | Reduce to mild-medium |
| Heavy cayenne | Half the amount or switch to smoked paprika | Paprika adds color and complexity |
| Unseeded jalapeño | Seeded jalapeño | Removing seeds and ribs removes most heat |

## General Principles for Substitution

**Match the function, not just the name:**
Think about why an ingredient is in the recipe. Bacon adds smokiness, fat, salt, and texture. Each of these can be addressed separately:
- Smokiness → smoked paprika, smoked salt, chipotle
- Fat → tallow, butter, avocado oil
- Salt → seasoning adjustment
- Texture → broiled beef bits if critical

**Taste as you go:**
Substitutions change flavor profiles. Avocado oil is more neutral than bacon fat. Adjust seasoning (salt, acid, umami) after making a substitution.

**When in doubt, simplify:**
Many pork-based flavor additions (bacon in soup, pancetta in pasta) enhance but do not define the dish. Removing them and adjusting seasoning often produces an excellent result without the violation.
